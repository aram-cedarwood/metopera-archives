Review 1:

Review in The New York Times:

Another concert was given at the Metropolitan Opera House last evening in the presence of a large audience. The principal enthusiasm of the evening was aroused by Mme. TrebeIli's rendering of the serenade from "Gil Blas." She also sang a rondo by Rossini. Mme. Valleria was heard with pleasure in Senta's ballad from "The Flying Dutchman" and in a duet from "Il Flauto Magico" with Signor Del Puente. M. Augier sang an aria from Adam's' "Le Chalet." M. Capoul gave a song from Gounod's "Mireille." Mme. Fursch-Madi sang an aria from "Don Giovanni." and Signor Del Puente rendered a romanza. The orchestra played Massenet's "Scenes Alsaciennes" and several other selections.
