Review 1:

Review in The New York Times:

Flotow's "Martha" was sung and acted at the Metropolitan Opera House yesterday afternoon, in the presence of a large audience, the cast including Mmes. Sembrich and Trebelli, and Signori Campanini and Novara. Signor Campanini's Lionello is familiar to New York opera-goers as a performance of marked originality, and it is quite within bounds to say that it has never been rivaled in this country. The great tenor was at his best yesterday-superb both in voice and action, and his labors were rewarded by the most enthusiastic applause. "M'appari" had to be repeated. Mme. Sembrich at the close sang Proch's "Air and Variations" with brilliant effect. Signor Vianesi led the orchestra
